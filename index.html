Interests and Background



I'm a biologist interested in understanding how ecological interactions are organized in nature and how such interactions shape the functioning of biological systems affecting diversity at local and regional scales (see Research Interests).

I graduated with a degree in Biological Sciences, and obtained a M.Sc. degree in Ecology (under the supervision of Sérgio F. dos Reis), at Campinas State University (UNICAMP), in Brazil.

I obtained a Ph.D. degree in Ecology at São Paulo University (USP) under the supervision of Paulo R. Guimarães Jr. I also developed part of my Ph.D. project as a visiting student at Paul Koch's lab at the University of California-Santa Cruz (UCSC).

Currently, I am a Postdoc at Tiago Quental’s lab (LabMeMe) in São Paulo University (USP) and I recently concluded a short-term Postdoc at University of California-Berkeley.
